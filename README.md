# **Codex**

**Codex** is a terminal-based worldbuilding and narrative authoring tool designed to make character creation, worldbuilding, and writing more immersive, structured, and enjoyable — without locking you into a specific workflow or platform.

Codex does not try to tell stories for you.
It gives you the tools to **enter** the worlds you are creating and shape them on your own terms.

---

## Vision

The vision of Codex is simple:

> **Make worldbuilding, character creation, and writing easier by helping creators immerse themselves in the worlds they are building.**

Codex is built around the idea that immersion is not just visual — it is cognitive.
When structure, presentation, and navigation feel natural, creative flow follows.

Codex achieves this by:

* Providing a guided, structured interface for creating characters and narrative elements
* Storing everything in human-readable Markdown
* Allowing deep customization through templates and ASCII presentation
* Supporting character-centric graphs that show how ideas connect within a world

Your level of immersion depends on how far you choose to take it.
Codex works out of the box, but advanced users can heavily customize layouts, formatting, and ASCII presentation to create richly styled “vaults” that feel alive.

You are always in control.
Codex provides the tools — **you provide the creativity**.

---

## What Codex Is (and Isn’t)

Codex **is**:

* A structured authoring tool for characters, worlds, and stories
* A terminal user interface (TUI) focused on immersion and clarity
* Markdown-first and plaintext-friendly
* Compatible with tools like Obsidian and static site generators
* Designed for both beginners and power users

Codex **is not**:

* A replacement for Obsidian or other note-taking apps
* An AI writing tool
* A game engine
* A cloud service or proprietary platform

---

## Who Codex Is For

Codex is for anyone who wants to build worlds and tell stories, including:

* Writers and authors
* Storytellers and narrative designers
* Worldbuilders
* Roleplayers and tabletop players
* People new to writing who want structure and guidance
* Experienced creators who want full control over formatting and presentation

Whether you want a simple character dossier or a deeply interconnected narrative archive, Codex adapts to your needs.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Creator Control

Codex never hides your data behind proprietary formats or services.
All content is stored in plain Markdown and simple metadata files that you fully own.

---

### 2. Structured, Not Restrictive

Codex provides templates, schemas, and guidance — not rigid rules.
You can follow them strictly, modify them, or ignore them entirely.

---

### 3. Immersion Through Presentation

ASCII layouts, custom templates, and clean terminal rendering are not gimmicks — they are tools for immersion.
How far you push this is entirely up to you.

---

### 4. Human-Readable First

Everything Codex creates should be understandable without Codex.
If the program disappeared tomorrow, your work would still be usable.

---

### 5. Local, Transparent, Open

Codex runs locally, requires no accounts, and is fully open source.
Trust comes from transparency, not abstraction.

---

## How Codex Works (High Level)

* You create or open a **vault** (a self-contained world or project)
* Codex guides you through creating characters and narrative elements using templates
* Your input is translated into clean, consistent Markdown
* Relationships between characters are tracked and visualized through character-centric graphs
* Vaults can optionally be opened in tools like Obsidian or published as static websites

Codex focuses on **creation and structure** — exploration and publishing are left to tools that already do those jobs well.
